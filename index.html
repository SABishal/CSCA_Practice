📚 CSCA Syllabus

Detailed syllabus for each subject – official CSCA examination topics.

📐 Mathematics

📐

Sets and Inequalities

  • Definition, operations, and representation of sets
  • Basic properties and solution methods of inequalities (e.g., quadratic and rational inequalities)
📈

Functions

  • Concepts and properties of functions (domain, range, monotonicity, parity, etc.)
  • Basic elementary functions (power, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions)
  • Sequences (general term formula and summation of arithmetic and geometric sequences)
  • Basics of derivatives and calculus (definition, geometric meaning, and simple applications)
📏

Geometry and Algebra

  • Plane analytic geometry (equations and properties of lines, circles, ellipses, hyperbolas, and parabolas)
  • Vectors and complex numbers (vector operations, basic operations of complex numbers)
  • Solid geometry (rectangular coordinate system in space and properties of simple solids)
📊

Probability and Statistics

  • Classical probability model and probability calculation
  • Numerical characteristics of data (mean, variance, etc.)
  • Basic concepts of normal distribution

⚛️ Physics

⚙️

Mechanics

  • Kinematics: displacement, velocity, acceleration, uniform acceleration motion, free fall motion
  • Newton's laws of motion and their applications
  • Momentum and impulse, law of conservation of momentum
  • Work and energy, law of conservation of mechanical energy
  • Circular motion and universal gravitation
  • Simple harmonic motion and mechanical waves
⚡

Electromagnetism

  • Electrostatics: Coulomb's law, electric field strength, electric potential
  • Direct current circuits: Ohm's law, series and parallel circuits
  • Magnetic field: magnetic induction, Ampere's force, Lorentz force
  • Electromagnetic induction: Faraday's law, Lenz's law
🔥

Thermodynamics

  • Kinetic theory of gases
  • Ideal gas equation of state
  • First law of thermodynamics
💡

Optics

  • Geometrical optics: laws of reflection and refraction
  • Physical optics: interference and diffraction
⚛️

Modern Physics

  • Photoelectric effect
  • Atomic structure
  • Fundamentals of nuclear physics

🧪 Chemistry

🧪

Basic Chemical Concepts and Calculations

  • Classification and state changes of matter
  • Chemical notation and equation writing
  • Solution concentration and pH calculations
  • Calculations involving the amount of substance
  • Application of the ideal gas law
⚗️

Properties and Reactions of Substances

  • Properties of common inorganic substances (elements, oxides, acids, bases, and salts)
  • Basic organic compounds (hydrocarbons and their derivatives)
  • Identification of redox reactions
  • Ionic reactions and testing methods
📖

Chemical Theories and Laws

  • Atomic structure and periodic law
  • Chemical bonds and intermolecular forces
  • Reaction rate and chemical equilibrium
  • Theories of electrolyte solutions
🔬

Chemical Experiments and Applications

  • Laboratory safety and use of apparatus
  • Preparation and identification of common gases
  • Methods for separation and purification of substances
  • Analysis of industrial chemical processes (e.g., ammonia synthesis)

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the CSCA exam

📋 Exam Overview
What is the CSCA exam? +

The CSCA (China Scholastic Competency Assessment) is a standardized test designed for international students who plan to pursue undergraduate degrees in China. It assesses language proficiency and academic readiness, serving as an important reference for university admissions and scholarship evaluations.

Is the CSCA exam mandatory? +

Yes, starting from the 2026/2027 academic year, all international applicants to Chinese Government Scholarship universities must take the CSCA exam as part of their application requirements.

What is the exam pattern for CSCA 2026? +
Subject Questions Duration
Professional Chinese 80 90 min
Mathematics 48 60 min
Physics 48 60 min
Chemistry 48 60 min

All questions are multiple-choice. No negative marking.

How often is the CSCA exam held? +

The CSCA exam is held 5 times per year in 2026: January, March, April, June, and December. Check the official website for specific dates.

What is the exam difficulty level? +

The CSCA exam is designed to test high school-level knowledge. Thorough preparation is recommended, especially since calculators are not allowed.

📝 Registration & Fees
How do I register for the CSCA exam? +

Registration is done online through the official CSCA portal at csca.cn. You'll need to:

  • Create an account on the portal
  • Fill in your educational details
  • Upload your passport information
  • Pay the registration fee
What is the registration fee? +
Single Subject 450 RMB
Two or More 700 RMB

Fees are non-refundable but can be transferred to a future exam date.

What ID is required for registration? +

Passport is the only acceptable ID for the CSCA exam. Make sure your passport is valid and matches the information provided during registration.

Can I get a refund if I don't appear? +

No, exam fees are non-refundable if you fail to appear. However, you may request a fee transfer to a future exam date with valid documentation.

📅 Exam Day
What documents do I need for exam day? +

Bring the following on exam day:

  • Admission Ticket (printed)
  • Valid Passport

Electronic devices and calculators are strictly prohibited.

Can I use a calculator? +

No calculators allowed. The CSCA math section is designed to be solved with mental math, algebraic manipulation, and scratch paper only. This policy applies to all subjects.

Where can I take the exam? +

CSCA is conducted primarily in a remote, home-based online format. Onsite test centers (computer-based or paper-based) are gradually being established in major source countries and regions.

What if my internet disconnects during the exam? +

Don't worry! The exam system auto-saves your answers. Simply reconnect to the test platform and continue from where you left off. Technical support is available during the exam.

How do I download my admission ticket? +

Log in to the official CSCA portal, go to the "Registration -> My CSCA -> Admission Ticket" section, and download it. The admission ticket becomes available a few days before the exam date.

📊 Results & Scoring
When are CSCA scores released? +
Online / Computer-based 7 working days
Paper-based 14 working days
How long are scores valid? +

CSCA scores serve as an important reference for university admissions and scholarship evaluations. You can retake the exam to improve your score within the application period.

Is there negative marking? +

No negative marking for wrong answers. Each correct answer earns points. We recommend answering all questions, even if you're unsure.

How many times can I attempt the exam? +

You can take the CSCA exam multiple times. Scores are valid as an important reference for admissions, and you can submit your highest score to universities.

📚 Preparation
What is the exam language? +
  • Professional Chinese - Chinese only
  • Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry - Available in both Chinese and English

You can choose your preferred language during registration.

What study materials are recommended? +

Recommended resources include:

  • Official CSCA study guides
  • Previous years' question papers
  • Online practice tests

The official portal provides approved study materials and resources.

How can I prepare for the no-calculator format? +

Tips for preparation:

  • Practice mental math daily
  • Learn multiplication tables up to 20
  • Practice estimation techniques
  • Solve problems manually on scratch paper

Our practice platform includes timed exercises to help you adapt.

Are there any prerequisites? +

There are no formal prerequisites. However, it is recommended that candidates have knowledge of Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry at high school level.

Is the CSCA certificate recognized internationally? +

Yes! The CSCA certification is recognized by all Chinese Government Scholarship universities and is a mandatory requirement for international applicants seeking scholarships to study in China.
